# Call Scoring Rubric Guide

This document is for the person responsible for defining how Argos scores sales calls. You don't need to be technical — your job is to define **what good looks like** for each scoring category. An AI will read the call transcript and apply your rubric to produce a 0–100 score per category.

---

## How This Works (Non-Technical Summary)

When a rep uploads a call recording, Argos will:

1. Transcribe the call into text
2. Send that transcript to an AI (GPT-4) along with **your rubric**
3. The AI reads both and scores the call based on your criteria
4. Scores are stored and displayed to managers and reps

**The AI is only as good as your rubric.** If your rubric is vague, scores will be inconsistent. If it's specific and grounded in real sales behavior, scores will be accurate and actionable.

---

## What to Write for Each Category

For each of the 7 categories below, you need to provide:

### 1. A Clear Definition
What is this skill? In 2–3 sentences, explain what it means in the context of a sales call.

### 2. What Good Looks Like (Score: 80–100)
Give 3–5 concrete, observable behaviors the AI can detect in a transcript. Be specific — mention things a rep would actually *say* or *do*.

> Example: "The rep opens by setting an agenda before the prospect can steer the conversation. The rep uses phrases like 'Before we dive in, here's what I want to accomplish today...'"

### 3. What Average Looks Like (Score: 50–79)
The rep shows the skill sometimes but inconsistently. What are the tell-tale signs of a middle-of-the-road performance?

### 4. What Poor Looks Like (Score: 0–49)
The rep is missing this skill or actively doing the opposite. What are the red flags?

### 5. Phrases or Patterns to Look For (Optional but Valuable)
If certain words or phrases signal good or bad performance, list them. The AI will use these as anchors.

> Example good phrases: "Before we get started...", "Is it ok if I ask you a few questions first?"
> Example bad phrases: "Whatever works for you", "Uh, so yeah, what did you want to talk about?"

## Tips for Writing Good Rubric Criteria

- **Be observable.** The AI can only score what it can read in a transcript. "Rep was confident" is hard to score. "Rep redirected when the prospect asked for price before discovery was complete" is scorable.
- **Anchor to real language.** Think about what your best reps actually say on calls. Pull phrases from real recordings if you have them.
- **Separate the score ranges clearly.** The gap between 80 and 50 should be obvious. If you can't articulate the difference, neither can the AI.
- **Don't make everything a 50.** If your criteria are too lenient, all reps will score similarly and the data becomes useless. Be willing to define what truly bad looks like.
- **Test it on a real call.** Once you've written the rubric, pick a call you've already listened to and ask yourself: would the AI score it the way you would? If not, adjust the language.
