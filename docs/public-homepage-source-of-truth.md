# Canonical public homepage direction

## Status

This is the single source of truth for the Argos public homepage narrative.

## Sales standard installation platform direction

Use the sales standard opener:

> Build a sales team that actually follows the playbook.

Then move into the coaching plus platform operating loop:

> call review -> scorecards and rubrics -> coaching flags -> training assignments -> roleplay practice -> manager dashboards

The homepage should not sell Argos as generic software or as a concierge call-review service. It should sell sales standard installation supported by the Argos platform: the standard gets installed across the organization, and Argos turns that standard into call reviews, scorecards, training assignments, roleplay practice, manager dashboards, and team coaching flags.

Core line:

> The coaching sets the standard. Argos reinforces it in the work.

## Canonical Flow

1. Hero: Build a sales team that actually follows the playbook.
2. Product screenshot carousel: show real Argos dashboard screens through buyer-facing coaching outcomes, not backend UI labels.
3. Coaching model: coaching only counts if it changes the next call; Argos reinforces it in the work.
4. How The Standard Gets Installed: install the sales standard, score real calls against it, reinforce it weekly.
5. Product loop: call review -> scorecards and rubrics -> coaching flags -> training assignments -> roleplay practice -> manager dashboards.
6. Role cards: For Owners, For Managers, For Reps.
7. Access: ask visitors to book a coaching walkthrough.

## Keep

- Headline: `Build a sales team that actually follows the playbook.`

- Public nav order: `Product`, `Coaching`, `Standard`, `System`, `Roles`, `Demo`.
- Problem card headline: `Coaching only counts if it changes the next call.`
- Problem card body: `Most teams leave the meeting nodding. Then the next call happens, old habits come back, and managers are stuck guessing what actually changed.`
- Product section rule: no standalone section title, no standalone section subtitle, and no card paragraph copy. The product carousel should move straight into the full Argos product loop with real product screenshots, concise slide labels, and line-broken benefit headlines.
- Eight-card product screenshot flow:
  - Dashboard: Know where to coach / before the week gets away.
  - Calls: Start with the conversations / your team actually had.
  - Scorecards: Score the moments / that make or break the sale.
  - Highlights: Pull out the moment / every manager should coach.
  - Training: Assign the fix / while the call is still fresh.
  - Roleplay: Practice the pushback / before the next live call.
  - Team: See who needs help / without hunting through calls.
  - Leaderboard: Track whether the standard / is spreading across the team.
- Product section headline format: split each card headline into two short, human lines like `Start with the conversations / your team actually had.`

- Standard section label: `How The Standard Gets Installed`
- Standard steps: `Install the sales standard`, `Argos scores real calls against it`, `Managers reinforce it every week`

- Product loop labels: `Call review`, `Scorecards and rubrics`, `Team coaching flags`, `Training assignments`, `Roleplay practice`, `Manager dashboards`
- Product loop card format: number, label, explanatory paragraph. Do not add amber proof lines like transcript highlights, score movement, manager flags, assignment status, roleplay queue, or dashboard signal.

## Design Notes

Use the warm Argos command aesthetic, but make the offer feel human and operational. The sales standard is the system. Argos is the reinforcement layer. The structure must stay concrete: calls reviewed, rubrics scored, coaching flags surfaced, training assigned, roleplay practiced, managers accountable.
