# Argos Product Context

register: product

## Product Purpose

Argos helps sales managers and reps turn real sales calls into scored evidence, focused coaching, roleplay practice, and a better next call.

The product loop is:

1. Call review
2. Scored evidence
3. Coaching moment
4. Roleplay drill
5. Next call

## Primary Users

- Sales managers who need to find coaching risk quickly, review evidence, and assign the next action.
- Sales reps who need to understand what happened on their calls, complete training, and practice specific skills.
- Admins who configure people, teams, rubrics, integrations, compliance settings, and account details.
- Executives who need a quick read on team performance and coaching risk without operating every workflow.

Future platform or agency staff belong in a separate platform space. Platform controls must not leak into tenant navigation.

## Product Standard

Authenticated Argos is an operational SaaS workspace. It should feel simple, direct, readable, and work-focused. The closest product references are GoHighLevel for practical navigation and page composition, Linear for quiet density and row readability, and Stripe for disciplined forms and settings.

The interface should disappear into the task. Avoid cinematic command-center styling, decorative dashboards, large hero panels, novelty controls, and dense piles of cards.

## Page Jobs

- Dashboard: show what needs attention now.
- Calls: find and review scored calls.
- Call detail: understand one call and create the next coaching action.
- Highlights: review saved coaching evidence and source calls.
- Upload: add a call recording and get to the generated detail page.
- Roleplay: practice a realistic scenario and review performance.
- Training: complete assigned learning or manage curriculum.
- Team: scan rep performance and open coaching profiles.
- Rep profile: understand one rep's score, trend, evidence, and coaching plan.
- Leaderboard: compare rank, score quality, volume, and improvement.
- Notifications: review product events and open the related record.
- Settings: configure the account, people, teams, permissions, integrations, compliance, and rubrics.

## Anti-References

Do not resurrect:

- "Forge command workspace" as a visual direction.
- Decorative glows, grid textures, bokeh, large ambient gradients, or cinematic dashboard panels.
- Marketing-page hero layouts inside authenticated pages.
- Repeated metric-card grids used as decoration.
- Nested cards inside cards.
- Uppercase label-heavy pages.
- Fake controls that do not change data or route state.
- Broad platform, agency, or super-admin controls inside the tenant app shell.

## Copy Rules

- Use direct product language.
- Prefer object-first labels: Calls, Team, Training, Settings.
- One short page description at most.
- Explain only what helps the user act.
- Avoid motivational copy, feature explanations, and repeated headings.
